How Approvals Work

Nothing publishes without your sign off

Your portal is the single source of truth for approvals. Every social post, every piece of website copy, every award submission, every publication pitch flows through one consistent approval workflow. This is the loop that keeps the engagement humming.

The monthly loop

  1. I draft the month ahead and it appears in your portal under Approvals.
  2. You open each item and see the full post, copy, and images. Takes five to ten minutes with a coffee.
  3. Approve the ones that are good to go. They publish on their scheduled date.
  4. If something feels off, hit Request Revision and tell me what to change. I rebuild it with your notes.
  5. If something is a hard no, hit Decline and I start fresh with a different angle.

The Questions You Might Have

Frequently asked

How do we reach you between monthly calls?

Text or email anytime. DK gets a direct line at 604.765.9270. Inside business hours I reply within a couple of hours. Outside hours, next morning. The portal captures everything that is not urgent so nothing slips.

Can we book shoots beyond the two included?

Yes. Additional shoots are at preferred partner pricing, billed per project, with a one to two week booking window. Good candidates are award worthy projects closing out mid cycle or new builds that need marketing coverage.

Who owns the photography?

You do, full commercial license on everything I deliver. No usage caps. Use it on the site, in ads, in printed material, submit it to publications, share it with collaborators. The only thing I retain is the right to include it in my own portfolio.

Who owns the rebuilt website?

You do. It is created under your Squarespace account with dk@gdnielsen.com as the owner. I am a contributor with administrator access for the build and ongoing updates. You can remove me with two clicks any time.

How do award submissions work?

Two submissions per year are included in the retainer. You pick the categories and the projects. I handle the submission package, photography selection, copy, and follow up with the submission body. Additional submissions are billed at preferred partner rates.

What happens if a project we wanted to shoot is delayed?

Shoots roll forward inside the contract year. If Forested Family Estate slips two months, the shoot moves two months. The two shoots per year commitment holds across the twelve month window, not the calendar.

What happens at the end of year one?

We have a formal review conversation, usually in person. You see the full year, I make the case for year two, we decide together. There is no auto renewal. The contract has a clean stop unless both sides want to continue.

What about the build documentation books Diane mentioned?

Not in year one scope, but on the menu. At roughly $7,000 per project, it is a separate deliverable we can add when a project is a clear candidate. Let us revisit after we have the first shoot under our belt.
